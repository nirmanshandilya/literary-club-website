Who We Are

A Space for Words
to Take Flight

"Literature is not just an art — it is the mirror of civilization."

The Literary Club of Government Engineering College Palamu, founded in 2024 with a singular belief: that every student carries a story worth telling. We are a community of readers, writers, poets, storytellers, and dreamers.

From intimate poetry evenings to college-wide literary festivals, we create spaces where voices — however quiet — are heard, celebrated, and preserved.

Whether you write for yourself or for the world, there is a chair for you at our table.

✒️

Creative Writing

Workshops, writing sprints, and mentorship sessions for fiction, poetry, essays, and beyond.

📚

Reading Culture

Monthly book discussions, reading circles, and a rotating library of curated titles for members.

🎤

Spoken Word

Open mic nights, slam poetry events, and storytelling sessions that bring the written word to life.

The River That Forgot Its Name

There was once a river in the foothills of a forgotten mountain that flowed without direction. Not because it was lost — rivers cannot be lost — but because the village it once nourished had stopped calling its name. And without a name, without someone to say it aloud at dawn, the river began to lose the thread of itself...
